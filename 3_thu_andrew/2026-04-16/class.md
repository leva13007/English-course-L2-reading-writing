### Text Features in Documents

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Acronyms** | Shortened form of a name/phrase using initials (e.g. NHS, ASAP) |
| **Abbreviations** | Shortened words (e.g. etc., approx., dept.) |
| **Subheadings** | Divide text into sections; help the reader navigate |
| **Captions** | Short text written under/beside images to explain them |
| **Imperatives** | Command or instruction form of verbs (e.g. "Turn off the engine.") |
| **Bullet points** | List items without full sentences; used for clarity and scanning |

### Idioms

| Idiom | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **to be on the same page** | to have the same understanding of a situation |
| **break a leg** | a way to wish someone good luck, especially before a performance |
| **hit the nail on the head** | to describe exactly what is causing a situation or problem |
| **let the cat out of the bag** | to reveal a secret or a surprise |
| **piece of cake** | something that is very easy to do |
| **cost an arm and a leg** | something that is very expensive |
| **under the weather** | feeling sick or unwell |
| **the ball is in your court** | it is your decision or responsibility to do something |
| **burn the midnight oil** | to work late into the night |

### Vocabulary Booster — Module 1

Covered three categories from **Cutting Edge Advanced Vocabulary Booster Module 1**:

- **Phrasal verbs** — verb + particle combinations with idiomatic meaning
- **Idioms** — fixed expressions whose meaning cannot be guessed from individual words
- **Miscellaneous / Collocations** — words that frequently appear together (e.g. *make a decision*, *heavy rain*)
