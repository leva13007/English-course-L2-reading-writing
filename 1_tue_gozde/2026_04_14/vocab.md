# Vocabulary & Grammar — 2026-04-14 / Report Writing & Line Graph Language

## Terms & Expressions

### From the *Cork* reading passage
- [ ] buoyant — able to float; light and able to stay up (cork is buoyant because its cells are filled with air)
- [ ] elastic / elasticity — able to spring back to original shape after being compressed
- [ ] insulating / insulation — preventing heat or sound from passing through (the bark insulates the tree)
- [ ] cellular structure — the internal arrangement of cells in a material
- [ ] replicate — to copy or reproduce exactly (technology has never succeeded in replicating it)
- [ ] flourish — to grow well and be healthy (cork oaks flourish in warm, sunny climates)
- [ ] thrive — to grow or develop successfully (the trees thrive in poor soil)
- [ ] sapling — a young tree
- [ ] harvest (noun/verb) — the process of collecting a crop or natural material
- [ ] strip (verb) — to remove bark or a layer from a surface
- [ ] lever away — to remove something by using a tool as a lever
- [ ] husk — the dry outer covering of a material
- [ ] granules — small particles or grains
- [ ] monopoly — exclusive control of a product or market
- [ ] compound — a substance made of two or more elements combined
- [ ] sustainable — able to be maintained without depleting natural resources
- [ ] biodiversity — the variety of plant and animal life in a particular habitat
- [ ] desertification — the process by which fertile land becomes desert
- [ ] account for — to make up or represent a proportion (accounts for roughly half of all production)

### Line graph vocabulary
- [ ] fluctuate / fluctuation — to change irregularly up and down
- [ ] peak (verb/noun) — to reach the highest point; the highest point
- [ ] level off — to stop rising or falling and remain constant
- [ ] plummet — to fall very quickly and suddenly
- [ ] soar — to rise very quickly to a high level
- [ ] surge (verb/noun) — a sudden large increase

## Grammar Focus

- [ ] Inversion with negative adverbs — *Hardly had I…*, *Little did he realise…*, *Seldom does it…*
- [ ] Past tense for graph description — use simple past for completed periods (*rose*, *declined*, *remained stable*)
- [ ] Noun phrases for trends — *a sharp increase*, *a gradual decline*, *a steady rise* (adjective + noun, not adverb)

---

## Typical Structures / Useful Patterns

### Introducing a graph
- [ ] The graph illustrates / shows / displays…
- [ ] The data indicates / reveals / suggests…
- [ ] It can be observed that…
- [ ] The most noticeable change is…
- [ ] The chart compares the trends in… over the period of…
- [ ] The data demonstrates a steady growth / decline in…

### Making comparisons
- [ ] While X was increasing, Y was declining…
- [ ] In contrast to…, …
- [ ] Compared to…, …
- [ ] On the other hand…
- [ ] Despite… / Although… / However…

### Adverb + verb collocations
- [ ] rose **sharply** / **dramatically** / **significantly**
- [ ] declined **gradually** / **steadily** / **slightly**
- [ ] remained **relatively** stable
- [ ] increased **marginally** / **moderately** / **considerably**

## Idioms / Collocations / Phrases

- [ ] account for — to represent a share (accounts for roughly half)
- [ ] in keeping with — consistent with, matching (*its image is in keeping with quality goods*)
- [ ] exercise in patience — something that requires a lot of waiting
- [ ] reach a high / low point — to hit the maximum or minimum value
- [ ] show a steady increase / decrease — to display a consistent directional trend
- [ ] experience a significant change — to undergo a major shift

---

## Advanced / C1+ Items

- [ ] **plummet** — more vivid than *fall*; implies speed and severity
- [ ] **soar** — more vivid than *rise*; implies speed and height
- [ ] **surge** — sudden and powerful increase (a surge in demand)
- [ ] **marginally** — by a very small amount (C1 adverb, more precise than *slightly*)
- [ ] **considerably** — by a large amount (formal alternative to *a lot*)
- [ ] Cleft structure for emphasis: *It was not until the 20th century that…*

## Notes

- [ ] "Sustainable" is used both in environmental contexts (sustainable material) and in business contexts (sustainable growth) — note the difference.
- [ ] Do NOT use "will" in conditional clauses after *if / provided that / as long as*.
- [ ] In report writing: use **headings** for each section and avoid contractions (*it will*, not *it'll*).
