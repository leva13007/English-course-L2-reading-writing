### Evidence & Explanations

**Q1 ❌ — NOT GIVEN** (answered: FALSE)  
The text only says: *"Its bark grows up to 20 cm in thickness."*  
There is no comparison with any other tree species. The passage neither confirms nor contradicts the claim → **NOT GIVEN**.

**Q2 ✅ — FALSE**  
Direct quote: *"that technology has **never** succeeded in replicating"* — the cellular structure has not been reproduced → **FALSE**.

**Q3 ❌ — FALSE** (answered: TRUE)  
The **25 years** refers to the time from planting to the **first** harvest, not between harvests:  
*"From the planting of a cork sapling to the first harvest takes 25 years."*  
The gap **between** harvests is approximately a decade:  
*"a gap of approximately a decade must separate harvests from an individual tree."*  
The statement is therefore incorrect → **FALSE**.

**Q4 ✅ — TRUE**  
*"If the bark is stripped on a day when… the air is **damp** — the tree will be damaged."*  
This implies bark must be stripped in dry conditions → **TRUE**.

**Q5 ✅ — TRUE**  
*"**No mechanical means** of stripping cork bark has been invented, so the job is done by teams of highly skilled workers."*  
Only manual (hand) removal is possible → **TRUE**.
