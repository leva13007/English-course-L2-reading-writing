### Grammar Check — SET 3

Fix the errors in these sentences:

1. The board was unanimous in their decision for approve the revised budget.
2. The proposal was discussed in details.
3. The report was submitted late due the fact that several figures needed verification.
4. He spoke as though the outcome was inevitable although the uncertainty.
5. The festival attracted less visitors than the organisers had projected.
6. She insisted to pay for the meal despite my objections.
7. The minister refused to comment, claiming of not being authorised to speak.
8. The committee questioned whether the proposal could be implemented without to increase costs.
9. The new guidelines were introduced with the aim to improving workplace safety.
10. He left the meeting abruptly, without he giving any explanation.
11. Even if he died last year, his family still misses him.
12. She completed the assignment in a shorter time as her classmates.

---

#### Corrected Sentences with Explanations

1. ✔️ The board was unanimous in their decision **to** approve the revised budget.  
_After "decision", use infinitive (to + V)._  
Після "decision" вживається інфінітив (to + V).

2. ✔️ The proposal was discussed in **detail**.  
_"In detail" is a fixed phrase — no article, no plural._  
"In detail" — сталий вираз, без артиклю і множини.

3. ✔️ The report was submitted late **due to** the fact that several figures needed verification.  
_"Due to" is a fixed prepositional phrase (not "due" alone)._  
"Due to" — стала прийменникова фраза.

4. ✔️ He spoke as though the outcome was inevitable **despite** the uncertainty.  
_Use "despite" or "in spite of" before a noun. "Although" requires a clause._  
Після "although" — підрядне речення; після "despite/in spite of" — іменник або герундій.

5. ✔️ The festival attracted **fewer** visitors than the organisers had projected.  
_Less + uncountable nouns; fewer + countable nouns._  
Less — з незліченними іменниками; fewer — зі зліченними.

6. ✔️ She insisted **on paying** for the meal despite my objections.  
_Insist + on + V-ing._  
Після "insist" — on + герундій.

7. ✔️ The minister refused to comment, claiming **not to be** authorised to speak.  
_After "claiming", use infinitive structure (not to be), not a gerund clause._  
Після "claiming" — інфінітивна структура.

8. ✔️ The committee questioned whether the proposal could be implemented without **increasing** costs.  
_After "without", use gerund (V-ing)._  
Після "without" — герундій (V-ing).

9. ✔️ The new guidelines were introduced with the aim **of improving** workplace safety.  
_"With the aim of" + V-ing — fixed phrase._  
"With the aim of" + герундій — стала конструкція.

10. ✔️ He left the meeting abruptly, without **giving** any explanation.  
_After "without", use gerund — not "without he giving"._  
Після "without" — герундій, без підмета.

11. ✔️ **Even though** he died last year, his family still misses him.  
_Even though = talking about facts; even if = hypothetical situations._  
"Even though" — факт; "even if" — гіпотетична ситуація.

12. ✔️ She completed the assignment in a shorter time **than** her classmates.  
_Comparatives use "than", not "as"._  
Порівняльні конструкції — "than", не "as".

### Formal vs Informal Language

#### Discussion

1. In what situations is it necessary to be formal with how you use English? Why?
2. What are the main differences between formal and informal language?
3. How do the British behave compared to people in your country? Are they more or less formal?

#### Identify and Transform

Rate the sentences below as **formal** or **informal/neutral**, then rewrite in a more formal style:

| Informal / Neutral | Formal Version |
|--------------------|---------------|
| I'm writing to tell you about a problem with my course. | I am writing to inform you of an issue regarding my course. |
| The teacher didn't explain things properly. | The teacher did not provide clear explanations. |
| I was really unhappy with what happened. | I was dissatisfied with the situation. |
| Can you fix this quickly? | I would appreciate it if this matter could be resolved promptly. |
| Lots of students are annoyed about this. | A number of students have expressed dissatisfaction. |

#### Informal → Formal Reference Table

| Informal | Formal |
|----------|--------|
| tell | inform |
| problem | issue |
| didn't explain | did not provide explanations |
| properly | clearly |
| really unhappy | dissatisfied |
| what happened | the situation |
| fix | resolve |
| quickly | promptly |
| lots of | a number of |
| annoyed | dissatisfied |
| about this | regarding this |
| can you | I would appreciate it if |
| I'm writing | I am writing |

### Complaint Letter: Matching Exercise

Match sentence beginnings (1–10) with endings (A–J):

| Sentence Beginning | Ending |
|--------------------|--------|
| 1. I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with… | A. …no explanation was provided. |
| 2. I was extremely disappointed to find that… | B. …I will be forced to take further action. |
| 3. Not only was the service poor, but… | C. …within the next seven days. |
| 4. What is particularly concerning is that… | D. …the product stopped working after one day. |
| 5. I had expected a higher standard; however… | E. …the service I received at your hotel. |
| 6. This is unacceptable given that… | F. …this was not the case. |
| 7. I would appreciate it if you could… | G. …also the staff were unhelpful. |
| 8. Unless this matter is resolved promptly… | H. …issue a full refund. |
| 9. I look forward to receiving your response… | I. …with the urgency it deserves. |
| 10. I trust that you will treat this matter… | J. …I paid a significant amount for this service. |

**Answers:**  
1 — E | 2 — D | 3 — G | 4 — A | 5 — F | 6 — J | 7 — H | 8 — B | 9 — C | 10 — I

### Writing Practice: Letter of Complaint (Hotel)

**Task:** Write a letter of complaint to the customer service manager describing problems with your room, restaurant food, and staff service. Suggest what you would like to happen next.  
Address: The Oaks, Redfern Way, Littlefield, RG11 9TX. Write at least 200 words.

**Example letter:**

---

<p align="right">
<br>
Jonathan Lecun<br>
Newport<br>
25/03/2026
</p>

The Oaks  
Redfern Way  
Little Field  
RG11 9PZ  

Dear Customer Service Manager,

I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with the room, food and service during my recent stay at The Oaks Hotel.

To begin with, I was extremely disappointed to find that the room was not clean upon arrival, as there was dust on the furniture and used towels left in the bathroom, which created a very poor first impression. In addition, the air conditioning was not working, making the room extremely uncomfortable, especially at night; as a result, I was unable to sleep properly during my stay. This is unacceptable given that I had expected a high standard of accommodation.

Turning to the food and service, I was equally dissatisfied. Not only was the meal I ordered cold when it arrived, but it also took a considerable amount of time for staff to respond after I complained. What is particularly concerning is that the staff were not helpful and did not handle the situation in a professional manner, which made the overall experience even more frustrating.

I would appreciate it if you could investigate these issues and provide appropriate compensation. Unless this matter is resolved promptly, I will be forced to take further action. I look forward to receiving your response within the next seven days. I trust that you will treat this matter with the urgency it deserves.

Yours faithfully,

[Your Name]

### Writing Feedback

| You wrote | You could say | Why this is better |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| offers much more convenience and opportunities | offers significantly greater convenience and a wider range of opportunities | more precise and formal phrasing |
| you can easily get around | you can navigate the city with ease | more natural collocation |
| many people still prefer | many people continue to favour | more advanced verb choice |
| deciding to live in a city is a trade-off | choosing to live in a city involves a trade-off | more natural structure |
| with it's own background soundtrack | with its own background soundtrack | correct possessive form |
