# Vocabulary & Grammar — 2026-03-25 (Formal Writing, Complaint Letters)

## Terms & Expressions

- [ ] dissatisfied / dissatisfaction — feeling that something is not good enough (I was dissatisfied with the service.)
- [ ] resolve — to fix or deal with a problem formally (I would appreciate it if this matter could be resolved promptly.)
- [ ] promptly — quickly and without delay
- [ ] regarding — about, concerning (I am writing regarding an issue with my order.)
- [ ] issue — a problem or concern (formal alternative to "problem")
- [ ] inform — to tell someone officially (I am writing to inform you of…)
- [ ] unacceptable — not good enough to be allowed
- [ ] a number of — several (formal alternative to "lots of")
- [ ] unanimous — fully agreed by everyone
- [ ] navigate — to find one's way (you can navigate the city with ease)
- [ ] favour (verb) — to prefer (many people continue to favour)
- [ ] trade-off — a balance between two things that cannot both be achieved fully
- [ ] appropriate compensation — a reasonable payment or other action to fix a situation
- [ ] take further action — to do more to resolve a problem, e.g. escalate a complaint

---

## Grammar Focus

- [ ] decision + to + V (infinitive): decision to approve → not "decision for approve"
- [ ] "in detail" — fixed phrase, no plural, no article
- [ ] due to + noun phrase (not "due" alone)
- [ ] despite/in spite of + noun/gerund (not a full clause)
- [ ] less + uncountable vs fewer + countable nouns
- [ ] insist on + V-ing: insist on paying
- [ ] with the aim of + V-ing: with the aim of improving
- [ ] without + V-ing: without giving (not "without he giving")
- [ ] even though (fact) vs even if (hypothetical)
- [ ] comparative: shorter than (not "as")
- [ ] Gerund after prepositions (without, on, of)

## Typical Structures / Useful Patterns

- [ ] I am writing to inform you of / to express my dissatisfaction with…
- [ ] I was extremely disappointed to find that…
- [ ] Not only was … but also…
- [ ] What is particularly concerning is that…
- [ ] I had expected a higher standard; however, this was not the case.
- [ ] This is unacceptable given that…
- [ ] I would appreciate it if you could…
- [ ] Unless this matter is resolved promptly, I will be forced to take further action.
- [ ] I look forward to receiving your response within the next seven days.
- [ ] I trust that you will treat this matter with the urgency it deserves.
- [ ] Turning to the [next issue], …

---

## Notes

- [ ] "Despite" and "in spite of" take a noun/gerund — never a full clause.
- [ ] "Less" is for uncountable nouns; "fewer" is for countable nouns.
- [ ] "Even though" describes a real fact; "even if" describes a hypothetical situation.
- [ ] Formal emails/letters use full forms (I am, did not) — no contractions.
- [ ] Complaint letter structure: intro → problem 1 → problem 2 → request for action → closing.
